A Short History of Large Language Models

1950s–1990s
Rule-based NLP — hand-crafted grammar rules & statistical models
2013
Word2Vec — words represented as mathematical vectors; meaning through context
2017
Google publishes "Attention Is All You Need" — the Transformer architecture that powers everything today
2018–2019
BERT & GPT-1/2 — pre-training on massive text corpora becomes the standard approach
2020
GPT-3 (175B parameters) shocks researchers — few-shot learning emerges; the scale era begins
Nov 2022
ChatGPT launches — 1M users in 5 days, 100M in 2 months. AI becomes a household word.
2023–2024
GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama — multimodal reasoning, coding, agents; every major tech company ships an LLM
2025–2026
Reasoning models, AI agents, deep integration into enterprise tools — AI moves from chat to action

Levels of AI Literacy

0
Unaware
Uses AI daily without recognizing it (spam filters, Maps, autocomplete). No mental model of how it works.
1
User
Intentionally uses AI tools — ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot. Knows how to prompt, recognizes limitations like hallucination.
2
Practitioner
Integrates AI into workflows via APIs or no-code platforms. Builds prompts, automations, and evaluates outputs systematically.
3
Developer / Engineer
Fine-tunes models, builds AI-powered applications, designs retrieval pipelines (RAG), evaluates model performance.
4
Researcher / Architect
Trains models, advances the state of the art, designs AI systems at scale, contributes to alignment and safety research.
Organizational goal: Move your team from Level 0 → 1, and identify Level 2 champions in each department.

AI Alignment

Ensuring AI does what we actually want — safely and reliably

What Is AI Alignment?

Definition: AI alignment is the challenge of ensuring that AI systems pursue goals and behave in ways that are consistent with human values, intentions, and well-being — even as they become more capable.

The Core Problem

  • AI systems optimize for what they're measured on, not necessarily what we want
  • Specifying human values precisely is extremely hard
  • A system can achieve a goal while violating the spirit of it
  • More capable systems can find unexpected "shortcuts"

Three Key Questions

  • Value alignment: Does it know what we want?
  • Technical alignment: Can it reliably do what we want?
  • Structural alignment: Do its incentives stay aligned as it scales?
A thermostat is trivially aligned. A system that can write code, plan actions, and learn from feedback is not.

The Alignment Spectrum

Near-Term

Today's Concerns

  • Hallucination & misinformation
  • Bias embedded in training data
  • Privacy violations & data leakage
  • Intellectual property questions
  • Overconfident outputs
Mid-Term

Emerging Challenges

  • AI agents acting autonomously
  • Consequential decisions made without humans
  • Workforce displacement at scale
  • Deepfakes & synthetic media
  • AI in weapons & surveillance
Long-Term

Existential Questions

  • Systems that pursue goals humans didn't intend
  • AI that can circumvent human oversight
  • Concentration of power via AI capabilities
  • Loss of meaningful human agency
Practical takeaway: Near-term alignment issues — hallucinations, bias, privacy — are your organization's problem today. They require policy and process now, not later.

How AI Labs Address Alignment Today

Technical Approaches

  • RLHF — Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback: humans rate outputs to guide the model toward preferred behavior
  • Constitutional AI (Anthropic) — model critiques its own responses against a set of principles
  • Red-teaming — adversarial testing to find failure modes before deployment
  • Interpretability research — understanding what's happening inside the model

Governance & Policy

  • Usage policies — prohibited use cases (weapons, CSAM, etc.)
  • Responsible scaling policies — capability thresholds that trigger safety reviews
  • Model cards & transparency — documenting what models can and can't do
  • Government collaboration — UK AI Safety Institute, US NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act
No silver bullet: Alignment is an active research area. Current models are much safer than two years ago — and the work continues. Your job is to use them with appropriate oversight.

Writing & Editing

  • Draft emails, memos, reports — give it the key facts, get a polished draft
  • Tone adjustment — make formal documents accessible, or professional documents from informal notes
  • Plain-language translation — convert legal or technical text for broader audiences
  • Proofreading & consistency — across large document sets

  • Transcription & summaries — Copilot, Otter.ai, Fireflies
  • Action item extraction — from meeting notes automatically

Content at Scale

  • Press releases & announcements — consistent branded voice
  • FAQ drafting — from source documents
  • Social media — platform-appropriate variants from a single brief
  • Translation — multilingual content without a full localization team
Real example: Feeding a 50-page annual report to Claude and asking for a 2-page executive summary with key metrics highlighted — in minutes, not days.

Always verify: AI can hallucinate citations, case numbers, or statistics. Build human review into any compliance-critical workflow.

Your Organization Needs an AI Policy

Why Now?

  • Employees are already using AI tools — with or without guidance
  • Sensitive data is being entered into consumer AI apps
  • Outputs are being relied on without verification
  • Liability exposure is growing for AI-assisted decisions
  • Regulatory requirements are emerging (EU AI Act, state laws)
A policy doesn't have to be restrictive. It can empower people by clarifying what's encouraged alongside what's prohibited.

What Happens Without One

  • Confidential client data submitted to ChatGPT
  • AI-generated content published without review
  • Inconsistent AI use creating unfair outcomes
  • No audit trail when AI output causes a problem
  • Staff anxiety about whether AI threatens their jobs
Samsung (2023) — engineers accidentally leaked proprietary source code to ChatGPT. The data was used for model training. Samsung subsequently banned ChatGPT internally.

AI Policy: Key Components

1
Acceptable Use Definition
Which tools are approved? Which use cases are encouraged, permitted, or prohibited?
2
Data Classification Rules
What data can never enter an AI tool? (PII, PHI, trade secrets, client confidential info)
3
Human Review Requirements
What categories of AI output require human verification before use?
4
Disclosure Standards
When must AI-generated content be disclosed to clients, regulators, or the public?
5
Training & Literacy
Minimum AI literacy requirements by role; ongoing training as tools evolve.
6
Governance & Review
Who owns AI policy? How often is it reviewed? Who handles incidents?
Start simple: A one-page policy that addresses data privacy and human review is better than a perfect policy that takes a year to write.

Key Takeaways

  • AI is already here. Your team uses it; your competitors use it. The question is whether it's governed.
  • LLMs are general-purpose reasoning tools — not just chatbots. The real value is in structured workflows.
  • Alignment matters practically. Hallucinations, bias, and data leakage are real risks that policy and process can mitigate.
  • Literacy enables agency. Organizations that build AI literacy across teams will outperform those that don't.

Your Next 90 Days

Week 1–2: Audit what AI tools your team already uses
Month 1: Draft a one-page acceptable use policy; identify data that can't go into AI tools
Month 2: Pick 2–3 pilot use cases (e.g., meeting summaries, first-draft memos); measure results
Month 3: Expand literacy training; designate AI champions per department
